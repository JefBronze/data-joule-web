The Problem

AI is exploding grid demand —
and flexibility can't be trusted.

~60%
average grid utilization

The grid is built for peaks it rarely hits — peaks that force costly, polluting peaker plants and block new connections.

#1
fastest-growing load: AI data centers

Yet no AI compute platform implements grid demand response. DOE (2024): "no examples of grid-aware flexible operation at data centers today."1

$0
trusted verification of flexibility today

Flexibility is self-reported and trust-based. If you can't independently verify what was delivered, you can't pay for it, trade it, or settle it.

Flexible load already exists. The problem is that utilities, aggregators, and load operators cannot independently verify who delivered flexibility, how much was delivered, and who should be paid. Without trusted verification, flexibility stays a promise — not a tradable asset.

1 U.S. DOE Secretary of Energy Advisory Board (SEAB), Recommendations on Powering AI and Data Center Infrastructure, 2024.

just as AI load takes off.

OpenADR 3.0 — the chosen standard2

The protocol U.S. and Canadian utilities are standardizing on for machine-to-machine demand response. First certified products shipped March 2025.

EPRI DCFlex3

Google, NVIDIA, PG&E, Duke, ERCOT and PJM are building AI-load flexibility on OpenADR 3.0. The market thesis is now an industry consortium.

AI factories as grid assets4

A 256-GPU cluster sustained 25% curtailment for 3 hours with no loss of AI quality of service (Nature Energy, 2025). NVIDIA & Emerald AI are productizing "flexible AI factories."

Hydro-Québec — our proving ground

A winter-peaking grid with active residential DR (Hilo) and the largest EV charging network in Québec (Circuit Électrique). Live integrations today.

The Solution

A flexibility platform on open standards —
with independent, auditable verification.

⚡

OpenADR 3.0 VTN/VEN

The international DR standard. Interoperable with any utility/ISO infrastructure, no vendor lock-in. Live today on Hydro-Québec and grid signals.

🔗

Independent verification

Each flexibility event is independently validated through a cryptographically auditable verification network before settlement. Every verified event generates a standardized digital receipt representing delivered flexibility.

🌐

Graded VEN response ladder

Not on/off. Four tiers from CPU throttle to full power cut, each with quantified power reduction and SLA impact — running on real hardware.

Why OpenADR 3.0 — the open international standard

✓

Interoperable by design — works with any DMS, BMS or SCADA without vendor lock-in.

✓

Native REST API (v3.0) — direct integration with modern systems; no proprietary middleware.

✓

Certified security — mutual TLS, OAuth 2.0; independent certification by the OpenADR Alliance.

Adopted across major regulated markets

CAISO — California PG&E Duke Energy ERCOT — Texas PJM — US East Google Nest Siemens

EPRI's DCFlex program adopted OpenADR 3.0 as the base protocol for AI-load flexibility. First certified products: March 2025.

💡

The meter remains the source of truth. Data Joule doesn't replace utility infrastructure — it adds an independent verification and settlement layer on top of the systems you already run.

Where We Fit

Aggregators orchestrate flexibility.
We verify and settle it.

The independent verification layer

Today, flexibility is self-reported and attested on trust. Aggregators like Voltus, Leap, Enel X and AutoGrid orchestrate it — Data Joule gives them and their utilities an independent way to verify and settle it.

Open, not proprietary

Built on OpenADR 3.0 and open source. Utilities and aggregators integrate without lock-in — the opposite of a closed black-box platform.

Neutral infrastructure, not a middleman

We don't compete for the end customer. We're the neutral verification-and-settlement infrastructure that aggregators and utilities both rely on.

Flexibility-first — starting with AI compute

Flexibility spans AI workloads, EV charging, batteries, HVAC and industry. We start with the fastest-growing, most flexible load — AI/GPU compute — which no platform exposes to the grid today.
